How to Hire a Freelance Web Developer (Without Getting Burned)

business · Mar 18, 2026 · by Suvo Dev · Sylhet, Bangladesh

Hiring a freelance web developer can feel risky — horror stories are everywhere. As a freelancer myself, here is exactly what I would check if I were on the hiring side.

1. Look at real, live work

Screenshots can be faked; live websites cannot. Ask for links to sites the developer built, then actually visit them. Check how fast they load on your phone.

2. Read reviews for patterns

One bad review means nothing; a pattern does. On Fiverr or Upwork, look for repeated phrases like "communicated well" or "delivered on time" — those habits will apply to your project too.

3. Test communication before paying

Send a short, specific message about your project. A good developer replies with clarifying questions, not just a price. Vague answers before payment become vague work after payment.

4. Agree on scope in writing

The #1 cause of failed projects is mismatched expectations. Before starting, both sides should have a simple written list: pages, features, revisions included, delivery date, and what counts as "done."

5. Start small

If the project is large, hire for a small first milestone — one page, one feature. You will learn more from one small delivery than from any portfolio.

Red flags to avoid

  • No live portfolio, only images
  • Promises "#1 on Google" — nobody can guarantee that
  • Demands full payment upfront
  • No questions about your business or goals
